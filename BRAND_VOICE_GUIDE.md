# Brand Voice Guide

## Overview

Brand Voice has been updated to use a **multi-select menu** with predefined, industry-standard options. This ensures consistency and makes it easier to define your company's personality.

---

## Why Multi-Select?

Instead of free-text entry, you can now select multiple attributes that combine to define your brand:

**Example:**
- A fintech startup might choose: `Professional` + `Innovative` + `Trustworthy`
- A creative agency might choose: `Playful` + `Bold` + `Creative`
- An enterprise SaaS might choose: `Authoritative` + `Data-Driven` + `Customer-Centric`

## How to Choose Your Brand Voice

### Step 1: Consider Your Industry
Look at the categories above and identify which ones naturally align with your industry standards.

### Step 2: Define Your Differentiation
What makes you different from competitors? Choose attributes that highlight this.

### Step 3: Select 2-5 Attributes
Don't overdo it! Pick 2-5 attributes that best represent your brand:
- **2-3 attributes** - Clear, focused identity
- **4-5 attributes** - More nuanced personality
- **6+ attributes** - Too complex, dilutes your message

### Step 4: Test for Consistency
Ask: "Would all these attributes work together in the same piece of content?"

## Tips & Best Practices

### ‚úÖ DO:
- Choose attributes that reflect how you actually communicate
- Select attributes your team can consistently deliver
- Pick attributes that differentiate you from competitors
- Use 2-5 attributes for clarity

### ‚ùå DON'T:
- Select contradictory attributes (e.g., "Formal" + "Casual")
- Choose attributes just because they sound nice
- Pick more than 6 attributes (too diluted)
- Ignore your industry's expectations

## Need Help?

### Can't Find the Right Attribute?
If you feel an important attribute is missing, consider:
- Is it similar to an existing option?
- Can it be represented by combining 2-3 existing attributes?
- Is it truly a voice attribute or more of a value/mission?

### Unsure Which to Choose?
Ask these questions:
1. How do we actually talk to customers?
2. What do customers say about us?
3. How do we want to be perceived?
4. What makes us different?
